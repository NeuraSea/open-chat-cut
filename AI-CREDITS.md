# NeuraSea AI Credits

NeuraSea AI Credits are optional service units for selected hosted AI features
in OpenChatCut. They are separate from the OpenChatCut software license. Local
models and bring-your-own-key (BYOK) providers remain available without buying
NeuraSea AI Credits.

## Preview offer

The planned Creator offer includes 100 NeuraSea AI Credits for each active
monthly billing period. Annual Creator licenses receive the same planned
monthly allocation rather than the full annual allocation in advance.

The hosted credit service and automated billing are currently in preview. A
Creator License purchased before launch grants the software rights stated in
[`CREATOR-LICENSE.md`](CREATOR-LICENSE.md), but does not create an immediately
redeemable credit balance. NeuraSea will identify the activation date before
charging separately for any hosted credit package.

## Intended coverage

Credits are intended for NeuraSea-hosted open-model capabilities such as:

- edit planning and content understanding;
- transcription, captions, embeddings, and semantic search;
- supported open-model image generation; and
- supported open-model text-to-speech.

The editor will disclose the estimated credit charge before a billable job is
submitted. The rate card may vary by model and workload and will be published
before paid activation.

## Exclusions

Credits do not include Seedance, Suno, or other paid third-party provider usage
unless an accepted order expressly says otherwise. Those providers require the
user's own key or a separately disclosed and approved charge.

Credits are service units, not money. Unless required by applicable law or
expressly stated in an accepted order, they are non-transferable, cannot be
resold or redeemed for cash, and unused monthly allocations do not roll over.

## Data and control

Using hosted AI requires sending the disclosed input to the selected endpoint.
OpenChatCut must show the destination and estimated charge before external
submission when approval is required. Generated results are downloaded into
the project's managed media library; a project must not depend on a temporary
provider URL.
